**6.1.** All club events require approval from the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs, via a completed club event proposal form.

**6.2.** The completed club event proposal form must be submitted at least two (2) weeks before the proposed event date.

**6.3.** Clubs may be penalized for failure to submit a club event proposal form. This may include, but is not limited to:
- Cancelling the event
- Ineligibility for reimbursement
- Suspension of other club privileges

**6.4.** By completing and receiving an approved event proposal submission, the club is registering their event with Saitsa and therefore shall be covered by Saitsa's insurance.

**6.5.** A club cannot plan an event without having or acquiring sufficient funding.

**6.6.** With permission from the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs are permitted to advertise and sell tickets to their events.

**6.7.** One (1) club member (not necessarily a club executive) shall formally assume responsibility to act as a Primary Event Organizer for the duration of an event, as identified on the event proposal form.

**6.8.** The Primary Event Organizer must be present for the entire duration of the event. This person cannot be a SAIT employee, member, non-club participant, or member of your parent organization.

**6.9.** Saitsa shall provide clubs with waiver forms for their members to sign if their event includes activities for which a waiver is deemed necessary by the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs. These include, but are not limited to the following:
- Transportation of members
- Extreme physical activity
- Events with alcohol

**6.10.** Off-campus activities on venues that require a contract or agreement are to be made in the name of the organizer (club executive or club name), not Saitsa. The club personally assumes responsibility for loss or damage in the event that they sign a contract or agreement with off-campus venues.

**6.11.** All room/space bookings on SAIT campus must be arranged by the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs.

**6.12.** In the event that a room/space is booked through a club's academic department/advisor, the club must:
- Provide booking information in the club event proposal form;
- Inform the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs, how payment for the room/space will be arranged.

**6.13.** Hosting an event or booking a space/room without approval from the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs may result in the following:
- **6.13.1** Club executives being held personally responsible for any damages or fees;
- **6.13.2** Referral of the violation to the Clubs' Committee for disciplinary action.

**6.14.** Should an unapproved event incur any costs or damages, Saitsa will not reimburse charges and will not approve funding requests for charges associated with damages.

**6.15.** All club events organized in a SAIT-managed area (i.e. 4Nines, Irene Lewis Atrium, etc.) must adhere to SAIT Policies and Procedures.

**6.16.** Saitsa may loan equipment for clubs to use for their events. The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs shall take reasonable steps to ensure clubs are aware of this service.

**6.17.** Saitsa may charge clubs an administration fee for processing equipment rental requests.

**6.18.** If equipment is required for an event, the request must be included on the event proposal form and is subject to availability.

**6.19.** Equipment rentals from Saitsa must be returned to Saitsa in their original condition the morning of the business day following the event, unless otherwise arranged with the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs.

**6.20.** Club funds may be withdrawn from a club's account for any Saitsa equipment or property that is damaged or stolen during an event.

### Hosting an Event with Food, Alcohol and/or Gambling

**6.21.** All on or off-campus events involving gambling activities, including 50/50 raffles or lotteries, are not allowed.

**6.22.** Alcohol and Club Funds: Clubs cannot purchase alcohol with funds from their club bank account. Funds can only be used to purchase the labor costs associated with hiring a bartender.

**6.23.** Admission to approved events with alcohol is restricted to club members and the guests that have completed the event waiver.

**6.24.** Promotion or advertising of a licensed function's activity that relates specifically to the drinking of alcoholic beverage may refer to the fact that it is licensed, but:
- **6.24.1** Must not specifically refer to the type, brand and/or manufacturer of the alcohol to be served;
- **6.24.2** Must indicate that admission is restricted to members and the guests of the sponsoring organization, group or individual; and
- **6.24.3** Must refer to the availability of non-alcoholic beverages. Positive slogans or icons promoting responsible drinking are to be visible in ads. Messages and/or slogans that promote overconsumption such as "cheap booze" and "get blitzed" are not permitted, as per AGLC advertising regulations.

**6.25.** Events that take place at a pub, restaurant or hotel that holds a liquor license and is the sole "provider" of socially responsible liquor service for the event will be referred to as **TYPE 1: Venue Operated Alcohol Service** and will have the following rules:
- **6.25.1** The event must be submitted two (2) weeks prior to the event date, and an in-person meeting between the Primary Event Organizer (PEO) and the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs should be arranged.
- **6.25.2** The club and alcohol providers must set up serving practices to ensure that patrons are not served past the point of intoxication.
- **6.25.3** Already intoxicated people cannot enter the club organized event.
- **6.25.4** Request Taxi Chits from the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs. At least one taxi chit is required for every 4 people attending. Attendees must be informed that taxi chits are available. If used, the taxi company invoices Saitsa and that money will come out of the club bank account.
- **6.25.5** The club will man a registration/check-in area where alcohol will be provided. The registration area is responsible for checking each attendee's ID to confirm they are of age; once confirmed, the attendee receives a stamp. Bartenders retain the ability and responsibility to reconfirm a patron's age as the situation warrants.

**6.26.** When a certificate of insurance is required, all insurance documentation and details must be submitted to the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs for endorsement at least two (2) weeks before the planned event. The certificate of insurance must include both the club and Saitsa as additional insured parties.

**6.27.** On-campus events with alcohol utilizing the SAIT Liquor license will be referred to as **TYPE 2: On-Campus Events with Alcohol Service** and will have the following rules:
- **6.27.1** Alcoholic beverages must be obtained through Commercial Services' food service provider — Hotel Arts.
- **6.27.2** Alcohol service and consumption may occur only at a pre-authorized location indicated on SAIT's current liquor license or at a location approved through the liquor license extension process, and only during the times specified on the permit for that location.
- **6.27.3** Already intoxicated people cannot enter the club organized event.
- **6.27.4** Request Taxi Chits from the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs. At least one taxi chit is required for every 4 people attending. Attendees must be informed that taxi chits are available. If used, the taxi company invoices Saitsa and that money will come out of the club bank account.
- **6.27.5** The club will man a registration/check-in area where alcohol will be provided. The registration area is responsible for checking each attendee's ID to confirm they are of age; once confirmed, the attendee receives a stamp. Bartenders retain the ability and responsibility to reconfirm a patron's age as the situation warrants.

**6.28.** When organizing an event on campus, all food must be ordered through SAIT's approved catering vendor.

**6.29.** Catering requests should be made at least one (1) month before the club event, and all clubs must adhere to the Quote Approval process.
