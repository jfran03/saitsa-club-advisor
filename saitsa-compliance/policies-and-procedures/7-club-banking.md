**7.1.** Once a club is registered, club executives will be responsible for establishing and managing the club's finances through the Saitsa-issued bank account.

**7.2.** Clubs are not permitted to maintain an additional or separate bank account to hold club funds.

**7.3.** Clubs will be required to submit to the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs, quarterly financial reports at the end of each term, detailing the revenue and expenses associated with their club issued bank account, and supported by a current bank statement.

**7.4.** Failure to submit the financial reports by the specified date at the end of each term will result in the discontinuation of a club's eligibility for funding from Saitsa, and referral of the violation to the appropriate committee for disciplinary action.

**7.5.** Saitsa shall be responsible for maintaining and managing the club reimbursement bank account.

**7.6.** Club executives in conjunction with another club member will be permitted to deposit cash or cheques into their Saitsa issued bank account.

**7.7.** Following the deposit, a detailed and comprehensive description of how the funds were generated must be provided in the quarterly financial report, and available upon request by the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs.

**7.8.** Withdrawals of club funds may only be made by club executives and the appropriate signing authorities in the form of an e-Transfer or EFT. Following a withdrawal, a detailed description of why the funds were withdrawn must be provided within the quarterly financial report, and available upon request by the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs.

**7.9.** Saitsa reserves the right to perform a financial audit of any club at any time.

**7.10.** In the case that a club ceases to be actively registered with Saitsa for two (2) consecutive fiscal years, any remaining club funds shall be forfeited to Saitsa and absorbed into the Student Experience department's budget.

**7.11.** Saitsa shall review records of registered clubs from the previous and current fiscal year to determine if a club failed to register or has disbanded. If a club is found to have not registered for two or more consecutive years, the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs shall notify the Manager, Student Experience that the club has disbanded, and the total funds the club had at the last time it was registered.

**7.12.** Saitsa shall attempt to contact the last known club executives to determine if the club has disbanded or failed to register. If no response is received within two (2) weeks, the club shall be assumed to have disbanded.

### Start-up Funding Reimbursement Program

**7.13.** Saitsa shall provide start-up funding for new club start-up, other club activities, returning club assets, or approved administrative expenses.

**7.14.** The club can spend up to **$500.00** for new clubs, and **$200.00** for returning clubs, upfront.

**7.15.** To apply for Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding, clubs must submit the Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding Application Form within one (1) month from the time when purchases and/or administrative expenses were paid.

**7.16.** Clubs may apply for Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding during the two (2) start-up funding intakes every month, in a fiscal year. Applications must be received prior to 12:00 p.m. of the start-up intake deadline to be considered for the current intake funding period.

**7.17.** Clubs can apply for the Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding by completing the Grant Intake Form available through the Student Clubs Online Portal.

**7.18.** Clubs are required to disclose any monetary or in-kind sponsorship that was received for the event to which they are applying for reimbursement funding.

**7.19.** Receipt or paid invoice (i.e. proof of purchase) must be included with the application.

**7.20.** Failure to submit all receipts and invoices upon application will result in the application being considered incomplete.

**7.21.** Incomplete applications will not be approved for reimbursement.

**7.22.** Funding is provided on a first-come, first-served basis and as a reimbursement only.

**7.23.** The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs shall review and approve eligible applications for Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding requests.

**7.24.** Saitsa shall provide Club Start-up Reimbursement Funding every 15th and 30th of every month.

### Tedi's Clubhouse Grant Program

**7.25.** Saitsa shall provide funding for clubs to support club activities that will benefit Saitsa Student Club members and the SAIT Community. These funds belong solely in the club bank account, not any members' personal account.

**7.26.** Saitsa registered student clubs are eligible to receive up to **$5,000.00** of funding per term, with terms running from July 1 to December 31 and from January 1 to June 30. Additional funding may be provided to a club each term, pending approval and review of the Manager, Student Experience.

**7.27.** Tedi's Clubhouse Grant Program **may cover:**
- **7.27.1** 100% of the total cost of the order, up to $5,000.00 of Hotel Arts catering for on-campus events.
- **7.27.2** 100% of the total cost of the order, up to $5,000.00 of Gateway Services equipment rentals for on-campus events.
- **7.27.3** 50% of the total cost of the order, up to $1,000.00 of club promotional/branded items.

**7.28.** Tedi's Clubhouse Grant Program **does not cover:**
- Direct donations to charities
- The purchase of alcohol
- The purchase of cannabis
- Repayment of loans
- Purchase of bid items for silent auctions
- SAIT hosted activities — activities must be hosted and run by the student club
- Items purchased prior to grant funding approval
- Gifts for club members
- Gifts for graduation and Year End Celebrations
- Subsidies for merchandise or attendance fees for SAIT Staff, Alumni and non-SAIT guests
- The purchase of luxury items and personal electronics not essential to club operations (AirPods, smartwatches, and other personal-use equipment or technology)

  - **7.28.1** Grant funding is subject to the approval of the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs, and is dependent on the merits of the proposal and availability of budgeted funds.

**7.29.** Funding is prioritized towards running events and initiatives that build an experience over the purchase of swag/merchandise. Clubs must meet eligibility requirements in order to be approved.

**7.30.** Applications must be submitted three months to at least one month prior to the club event/project start date. This timeframe ensures adequate time for approval and funds transfer processes.

**7.31.** Applications must be submitted at 12:00 p.m. of the grant funding intake deadline to be considered for the current intake funding period.

**7.32.** Clubs can apply for the Tedi's Clubhouse Grant Program by completing the Grant Intake Form available through the Student Clubs Online Portal.
- **7.32.1** Applications should have budget estimates that include pricing screenshots or initial quotes to substantiate the budget estimates.

**7.33.** Each activity/project is its own separate grant proposal. Clubs may submit more than one application per intake period.

### Grant Program Review Committee

**7.34.** The Grant Program Review Committee, together with the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs and Student Experience, Clubs Assistant will evaluate all applications for approval.

**7.35.** The Grant Program Review Committee is constituted by assembling a varied group of individuals chosen from the pool of interested candidates. Should there be a lack of interest from the general student body, the Committee will seek the involvement of the Assistant Manager, Student Experience.

**7.36.** The committee aims to include representatives from various clubs and backgrounds to ensure a balanced review process.

**7.37.** The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs announces the upcoming grant review cycle and the need for committee members.

**7.38.** The announcement is shared through official communication channels such as emails and Saitsa Clubs Newsletters.

**7.39.** Interested Saitsa club executives and members are encouraged to express their interest in being part of the Grant Program Review Committee.

**7.40.** Interested individuals submit their expressions of interest by sending an email to the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs at **saitsa.clubs@edu.sait.ca** with the subject line: *"Request to Participate: Grant Review Student Committee."*

**7.41.** The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs reviews the expressions of interest received and verifies that they meet the eligibility criteria, ensuring the individuals are either club executives or members.

**7.42.** Those who meet the criteria are acknowledged with a confirmation email that outlines the next steps.

**7.43.** The selected committee members are invited to an orientation session where they are briefed on the review process, evaluation criteria, and guidelines.
- **7.43.1** They are provided with information about maintaining fairness, confidentiality, and professionalism throughout the review process.

**7.44.** The Grant Program Review Committee will meet approximately one week after the grant application deadline, and decide whether to award full, partial, or to deny the application.

**7.45.** Any Saitsa club executive or Saitsa Club member can participate in the grant review, provided that their club does not have an application submitted for that grant intake.

**7.46.** The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs retains the final authority to approve, deny, or request revisions to any application, to ensure all funding decisions align with Saitsa's policies, eligibility criteria, and the overall intent of the grant program.

---

### Grant Review Process

**7.47.** Grant applications are distributed among the committee members for review. Each application is typically reviewed by multiple committee members to ensure unbiased evaluations.

**7.48.** Committee members evaluate applications based on predefined criteria such as alignment with club goals, feasibility, and potential impact.

**7.49.** The committee convenes to discuss the applications and share their evaluations.

**7.50.** Deliberations are held to collectively determine which applications should receive funding based on the evaluations and discussions.

**7.51.** The committee has the right to deny funding if the following occurs:
- There is insufficient or missing information in the application. All student club applicants must be specific and correct in calculating the requested amount.
- The club did not fulfill eligibility requirements by the grant deadline.
- There is no Event Proposal submitted or the Event Proposal was unapproved.
- The project/activity is considered less beneficial than other applications in an oversubscribed grant intake.
- The club is not in good financial standing.
- The request is not something that Saitsa would approve (cannabis, alcohol, etc.).

**7.52.** After thorough discussions, the committee reaches a consensus on which grant applications to approve and allocate funding to.

**7.53.** The decisions are made with a focus on the quality of the proposed initiatives and their potential contribution to the student community. The committee's decisions are communicated to the applicants.

---

### Following Grant Decisions

**7.54.** The Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs will notify successful applicants about the grant they will receive, along with any conditions or reporting requirements, and will indicate the application close-out date.

**7.55.** Saitsa shall deposit the approved amount to the Saitsa Issued Club Bank Account within 2 weeks from the grant application deadline.

**7.56.** All receipts relating to the grant must be submitted to the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs on or before the close-out date indicated on the grant approval email, or the club will be held financially responsible.

**7.57.** Lost receipts will not be honored. Only approved expenses with receipts that have been submitted to the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs will be covered by the grant.

**7.58.** Clubs who have received external monetary sponsorship for an event and/or project should fully utilize the external monetary sponsorship funding first, before applying for the grant.

**7.59.** Expenses incurred that are not listed in the grant funding request will not be covered by the grant. These expenses are the club's responsibility.

**7.60.** Clubs that come under budget do not get to keep unused funds in their account. An automatic reclaim of unused grant funds will occur after the close-out date.

**7.61.** Clubs must adhere to attendance tracking for grant-funded events.

**7.62.** If the date of a club event involving grant funds changes, it is the responsibility of the club to notify the Student Experience Coordinator, Clubs for the grant close-out date to be changed.
